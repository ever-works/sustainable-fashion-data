## Overview

Nau was founded in Portland in 2005 with the goal of proving that business could be a force for environmental good. The brand is now owned by South Korean outdoor company Black Yak but continues its focus on sustainable, versatile performance-lifestyle clothing.

## Materials

- Recycled polyester
- Organic cotton
- TENCEL lyocell
- Ethically sourced Merino wool
- PFC-free coatings for water repellency instead of persistent chemical treatments used by most outdoor brands

## Key Features

- Versatile designs suitable for both city commutes and outdoor activities
- Uses recycled nylon for key pieces like the Aeroshell Hooded Shirt
- Avoids PFC-based water repellents common in the outdoor apparel industry
- Focus on durable, multi-purpose garments that reduce the need for multiple specialized pieces

## Notable Products

- **Latitude Crew Pullover**: A versatile performance-lifestyle pullover
- **Aeroshell Hooded Shirt**: Made from recycled nylon, designed for city commutes and weekend hikes

## Sustainability

- Prioritizes recycled and organic materials across its collections
- Uses PFC-free DWR (durable water repellent) coatings
- Emphasizes garment longevity and versatility to encourage fewer, more durable purchases
- Owned by Black Yak since acquisition, maintaining its sustainability focus under new ownership

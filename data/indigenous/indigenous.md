## Overview

For over 24 years, Indigenous has partnered with artisan communities to create sustainable, organic clothing that is impeccably handmade and unique. The brand preserves traditional textile techniques while providing fair wages and economic opportunities to skilled artisans.

## Features

- Over 24 years of fair trade partnerships
- Handmade using traditional techniques
- Handknitting and handweaving methods
- Machine handknitting for certain pieces
- Organic materials throughout
- Fair trade certified production
- Artisan empowerment programs
- High-quality, unique garments
- Sustainable material sourcing

## Sustainability Practices

Indigenous works directly with artisan cooperatives, primarily in South America, to create clothing using heritage craft techniques including handknitting, handweaving, and machine handknitting. All garments are made from organic materials and produced under fair trade standards, ensuring artisans receive fair compensation and work in safe conditions.

## Product Range

Offers sweaters, cardigans, dresses, tunics, wraps, accessories, and home textiles featuring intricate handcrafted details and organic fibers like alpaca, pima cotton, and organic cotton.

## Pricing

Mid to premium pricing reflecting artisan craftsmanship and organic materials, typically $80-300 per garment.

# Louis Vuitton

## Overview
Louis Vuitton is a luxury fashion house founded in 1854, headquartered in Paris, France. As part of LVMH, it has implemented the LIFE 360 sustainability strategy aligned with the 1.5°C climate pathway.

## Sustainability Features
- **LIFE 360 Strategy**: Aims to limit global warming below 1.5°C through science-based targets.
- **Raw Materials**: By 2026, 100%:** of strategic raw materials will be certified to guarantee preservation of ecosystems and water resources.
- **Carbon Footprint**: Target to reduce carbon footprint by 55% by 2030.
- **Creative Circularity**: Designs products made to last, offers repair services, and explores circular business models.
- **Supply Chain Transparency**: Works on traceability and responsible sourcing.

## Pricing
Louis Vuitton products are luxury goods with pricing varying by product line; specific pricing is not publicly disclosed as a sustainability metric.

## Use Cases
The brand's initiatives serve as a model for luxury fashion seeking to integrate circularity, responsible material sourcing, and climate action into high-end products.

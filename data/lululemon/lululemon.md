# Lululemon

## Overview
Lululemon is an athletic apparel brand founded in 1998, headquartered in Vancouver, Canada. The company emphasizes circularity and sustainable material innovation.

## Sustainability Features
- **Like New Program**: Buy-back initiative for used gear; profits (or 2% of revenue, whichever is higher) fund sustainability initiatives.
- **Preferred Materials Goal**: By 2030, 99% of products to contain at least 25% preferred materials by weight.
- **Innovative Materials**: Actively develops and invests in new sustainable materials.
- **No Carbon Offsetting**: Focuses on direct emissions reductions via product/material innovation, manufacturing, and transportation improvements.
- **Extended Producer Responsibility**: Designs for durability and recyclability.

## Pricing
Lululemon products are positioned in the premium athletic wear segment; pricing varies by item (e.g., leggings $98-$148, jackets $128-$298). Sustainability initiatives are integrated into operational costs.

## Use Cases
The brand's approach demonstrates how athletic wear companies can implement circular business models, material innovation, and transparency without relying on offsets.

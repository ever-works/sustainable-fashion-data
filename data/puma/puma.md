# Puma

## Overview
Puma is a global sportswear brand founded in 1948, headquartered in Herzogenaurach, Germany. The company has launched PUMA Vision 2030 with ambitious climate and environmental goals.

## Sustainability Features
- **Emissions Reduction Targets**: By 2030, reduce Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 90% and Scope 3 emissions by approximately one third.
- **Renewable Energy**: Commitment to use 100% renewable electricity across operations.
- **Zero/Low-Emission Fleet**: Transitioning company car fleet to zero- or low-emission vehicles where charging infrastructure permits.
- **Water Security & Forests**: Recognized by CDP for efforts in water security and forest conservation.
- **Climate Transparency**: Public reporting and third-party validation of environmental performance.

## Pricing
Puma products span athletic footwear, apparel, and accessories; pricing varies by product line (e.g., sneakers $60‑$200, apparel $30‑$150). Sustainability investments are integrated into overall business operations.

## Use Cases
Puma’s Vision 2030 illustrates how major sportswear brands can set science‑based emissions targets, transition to renewable energy, and address water stewardship while maintaining performance and style.

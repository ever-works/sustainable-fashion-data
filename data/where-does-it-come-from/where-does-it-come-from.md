## Overview

Where Does It Come From is a UK fair trade company selling organic cotton shirts made from cotton grown in India and spun and woven by hand by workers in a local cooperative. The brand is part of the khadi movement promoted by Gandhi to support traditional skills and empower rural workers, particularly women.

## Sustainability Practices

- Organic cotton grown, spun, and woven by hand in local Indian cooperatives
- Virtually no carbon used during production
- Low water techniques and eco-friendly dyes
- 100% plastic-free with wood or shell buttons
- Recycled tissue and double thickness reusable posting envelopes
- Shirt boxes from recyclable cardboard
- Fairtrade certified and member of British Association of Fairtrade Shops
- Works with Fairtrade certified supplier Moral Fibres for children's denim range
- Collaborates with charities on fundraising initiatives

# Nike

## Overview
Nike is a global sportswear brand founded in 1964, headquartered in Beaverton, Oregon, USA. The company has integrated circularity and waste reduction across its value chain.

## Sustainability Features
- **Waste Diversion**: All waste in Nike's extended supply chain is diverted from landfill; at least 80% is recycled back into Nike products or other goods.
- **Recycled Content**: Footwear incorporates at least 25% recycled materials.
- **Refurbishment Programs**: Collects eligible returns, open-box footwear, vintage, and deadstock items for refurbished resale.
- **Recycling & Donation Services**: Offers consumer take‑back for recycling and partners on donation initiatives.
- **Circular Design**: Uses reclaimed materials to create newly designed products.

## Pricing
Nike products span a wide range; pricing varies by product category and collection. Sustainability efforts are embedded in overall operations.

## Use Cases
Nike’s model illustrates how large sportswear brands can scale circularity through waste diversion, recycled material use, and product refurbishment while maintaining performance and style.
